Android Development

With this player/recorder you could find online radio stations, keep them in favorites list, record simultaneously multiple radio streams and play back recordings.

All you need for this is the connection of your phone or tablet to Internet. WiFi is preferable due to data consumption (most web radio stations use 128 kbit/s or even more).

You could play recorded programs with this application (no internet connection needed) or with your favorite media player.

When recording the streams, they are cut and saved in partial files (by default 8mb, but you could change that setting). Every saved partial file has MP3 tag and become immediately visible by Android OS and other media player applications.

Android's Back button does not close the application. It just disposes visualization components, but keeps player alive. Application's "Spidola" image is seen in the Notification area until Exit selected in the menu or Close button touched in the opened notification (which is used as Play/Pause control for the radio/player).
